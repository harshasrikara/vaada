Our Story

In 2022, our founder realised the lack of healthcare expertise in the Local Conference of Youth. Fueled by a resolute spirit, she decided to instigate change. With support from fellow youth-led health organizations, she aimed to bring together future healthcare professionals to address inequalities and the struggles faced by vulnerable population groups.

What started as a resolution to host one health camp, stretched to the birth of VAADA.

A dedicated group initiated outreach efforts to mitigate the health concerns in the population affected by the Yamuna floods. VAADA complemented national efforts by rallying to secure medicines and medical equipment, aiding Yamuna Flood Survivors. On Independence Day, VAADA held its first health camp, assisting 1200+ individuals with medical care, nutrition and essential supplies. VAADA aspires to foster Pro Planet People by advocating for Universal Health Coverage.

Why attend UNDP COP28?

Because we bring a unique perspective for the mission LiFE program by looking at through the health lens. Promoting health is vital for a sustainable lifestyle and a healthier environment.

Healthy Choices = Sustainable Choices

Encouraging healthy habits like plant-based (millets), locally-sourced diets and exercise (yoga, pranayama) leads to sustainable choices with lower environmental impact.

Reducing Waste

Health advocacy promotes responsible waste disposal, reduced plastic use, benefiting the environment.

Active Transportation

Promoting physical activity through walking and cycling not only improves health but also reduces carbon emissions.

Protecting Natural Resources

Health campaigns raise awareness about water conservation and clean sanitation, preserving ecosystems.

Climate Resilience

Teaching about climate-related health risks prepares communities for environmental challenges.

Community Engagement

Involving communities in health decisions fosters environmentally friendly behaviors and reduces poverty.

Partnerships for Impact

Collaboration between health and allied sciences (including indigenous and traditional medicine such as AYUSH) and environmental organizations amplifies efforts to address interconnected issues.

Long-Term Well-Being

Prioritizing health leads to a lasting commitment to environmental protection and sustainability. Investing in health is investing in a sustainable, resilient future for both individuals and the planet.
